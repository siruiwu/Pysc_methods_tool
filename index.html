About the project

Why this site exists

Many students do not struggle because they have never heard of t-tests, ANOVA, or regression. They struggle because they do not know how to move from a research question to a method that fits their study.

Method Compass is designed as a calm, supportive educational website for psychology and social science students. It teaches method choice through guided structure, realistic examples, and direct attention to common confusions.

This is not meant to be a full statistics encyclopedia or a software tutorial site. Its job is to help beginners know where to start, what options make sense, and what to learn next.

Questions students often ask

FAQ

Is this site for beginners?

Yes. The language stays plain and the flow is designed to reduce overwhelm.

Does this replace a statistics course?

No. It helps you narrow likely methods and understand the reasoning behind them.

Can this tell me exactly what method to use?

Not with complete certainty. It gives structured educational guidance and shows what to check next.

What if I still feel unsure?

Use the Resources section, compare nearby methods, and treat the shortlist as a starting point for discussion with an instructor.
